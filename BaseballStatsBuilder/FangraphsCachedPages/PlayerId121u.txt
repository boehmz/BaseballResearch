The hard-throwing righty was largely mediocre in 30 big league innings, with most of them coming in relief. Mitchell has had some success as a starter in the minors the last couple of years, but his future is likely in the bullpen. He undeniably has the stuff to be an effective late-inning reliever, but spotty command has lead to underwhelming performances in the show. That’s good news for Mitchell, who currently slots in as the club’s sixth starter. With no minor league options remaining, though, he’d have to pass through waivers to be sent to the minors so if he’s not in the starting rotation he’ll likely be headed to the bullpen as a long reliever. Mitchell, 25, has a strong fastball but isn’t a strikeout pitcher. He relies on control and his cutter to induce weak contact and generate a ton of ground ball outs; he does an excellent job of keeping the ball in the park. Mitchell doesn’t project to be a huge fantasy contributor in 2017 due to his lack of strikeouts and lack of guaranteed role -plus both Luis Cessa and Chad Green pose competition for starts. However, you just might catch lightning in a bottle for a short period of time with him during the coming year if he does happen to make his way into the starting rotation for the Yankees. (Marc Hulet)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Mitchell doesn’t produce impact numbers but he has the stuff to be a reliable big league pitcher - either as a starter or reliever.
