While sitting in the high 80s as an amateur, Boyd developed greater arm speed as a member of the Blue Jays system. He began touching, if not sitting in, the mid-90s. This newfound velocity, in concert with strong command and a collection of adequate secondaries, allowed Boyd to record elite statistical indicators in the minors. It would be wrong to suggest, however, that he experienced immediate major league success. Splitting the 2015 season between Toronto and Detroit, Boyd recorded below-average fielding independent metrics and even worse batted-ball numbers. The result: a 7.53 ERA in nearly 60 innings. Upon re-joining the Tigers rotation in late May of 2016, however, Boyd fared better, recording about a win in roughly 100 innings of work. That sort of production renders him a decent No. 4 starter. He's more likely to begin the 2017 season at Triple-A Toledo than in Detroit. Given a role, however, he should be able to provide league average innings.
