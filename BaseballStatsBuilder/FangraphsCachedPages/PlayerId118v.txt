In 2015, however, he was much more impressive, raising his strikeout rate while also cutting his walk rate in half. Walker solved one problem in 2015, but he still has an obstacle to overcome: homers. Walker was never overly homer-prone in the minors, but he wasn’t all about keeping the ball in the yard, either. Perhaps a couple of the homers were due to poor circumstances and his rate will decrease in 2016, but it has to come down from quite a high number, and an incremental improvement will still lead to less than ideal results. Entering just his age-23 season and not having pitched exclusively until becoming a pro, there’s a good chance Walker can figure things out and become more than just a kid with great stuff and middling results. Walker has the upside of an ace, but not in 2016, so pay for him to be a middle of the fantasy rotation arm and hope for more. Those pitches are fine for the most part -- it's the rest of the package that produces problems. He's got a cutter that goes in and out of his life due to command problems, and the curve is really just a stolen strike pitch. He's failed to develop a third go-to pitch, and did we mention the command problems? If you're an optimist, you say the command problems came from foot issues -- he ended up having 10 (!) bone spurs removed from his right foot in the offseason -- but then you'd have to wonder why his delivery and/or athleticism has produced so many bone spurs over time. Walker is starting with two positives in his corner -- velocity and the splitter -- but he has a lot of work left to do to become the ace Arizona hopes he can be. Be careful.
