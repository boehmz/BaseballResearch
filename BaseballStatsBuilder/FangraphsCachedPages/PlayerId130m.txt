                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Nola’s value looks like it's all over the place going into 2016. Some owners will think there is room to grow while other could be disappointed after 2015. Others may see him as a Phillies pitcher and just stay away. Looking at his Steamer projection, I think it values him correctly going into 2016 with 7.5 strikeouts and 2.3 walks per nine. Some pitchers with similar numbers from 2015 are Jose Quintana, Collin McHugh, and Sonny Gray. These pitchers are not front line aces, but are serviceable in all leagues. He has a nice mix of pitches and results from them so far. The one item he is missing is an above average swing-and-miss fastball. Besides decent stats, he should throw 200 innings. Last season, he totaled 186 innings between the majors and the minors. Adding 30 innings to a pitcher each season is common, so I could see him throwing a full season. The innings may have worn him down and there may be a bit of upside in a fresh season. His Baseball America and MLB.com preseason prospect reports had his fastball at 93-95 mph. Early minor league reports were at 90-94 mph. In the majors he was at 88-92, averaging 90 mph. With the lower velocity, his strikeout numbers may have dropped. I think he is good, just not great. (Jeff Zimmerman)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Aaron Nola looks like he'll be taken in every fantasy league, but won’t be a huge difference maker. He should be a source of good but not great stats for years to come.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>

His sinker has below-average spin… and great sink as a result. Only Charlie Morton and Marcus Stroman got more drop on their two-seamers last year. Then you add in the curve, which stood out because only Sonny Gray threw a curve with more horizontal movement (if you count Corey Kluber's pitch as a slider). The change is comfortably Nola's worst pitch, but it also got more drop last year than any other change thrown by a starter, and only a pair of sidearmers were ahead of him on that list at all. At barely over 90 mph, Nola does have something going against him. Oh, that and injury, since a poor stretch last year led right into an early exit due to elbow soreness. But plus command and plus movement on a three-pitch arsenal should be enough to make him as good as his peripherals suggested he should be last year. If he's healthy, expect numbers commensurate with a fantasy number two. Any increase in velocity could push him to ace status. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>He doesn't throw hard, and his ball-in-play results last year covered up strong peripherals. If that makes Nola cheaper than a fantasy two or three next year, jump at the chance to buy into a surplus suggested by both his projections as well as his stuff.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
