Things did not go exactly as planned, as Pierzynski outhit the youngster and ended up running away with the starting role with his hot bat. In 436 plate appearances spanning 113 games, Pierzynski hit .300 for the first time since 2009, and his 114 park and league adjusted OPS was his second-best mark since '03. Pierzynski's strong debut with Atlanta resonated with the club, as he signed another one year deal with the Braves this past winter. The 39-year-old will enter the season just like he did last year: intended to mentor and support the Braves' catcher of the future, whoever it may be.
