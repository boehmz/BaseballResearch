However, there are a lot of question marks from the top of the house (Zack Greinke, Shelby Miller) down. That could be good news for Shipley, who is coming off of a lackluster freshman campaign. The 24-year-old hurler allowed too many hits and too many home runs in his debut while not missing nearly enough bats. Shipley's velocity was down a bit and his fastball command wasn't great. He'll need that command and an improved changeup to help him combat left-handed batters who teed off on him with a HR/9 rate of 2.43, compared to right-handed hitters at 0.92, which is much closer to league average. Shipley has potential to eventually turn into a reliable number three or four starter. He's only been pitching full-time since his college days after giving up hitting (and playing as a two-way player), and he's one of the more athletic pitchers in the majors. He'll likely be best served by spending a little bit more time in Triple-A before settling into the Diamondbacks rotation. (Marc Hulet)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Shipley's first big league season didn't go quite as hoped -- and it didn't help that he seemed to be pitching with a little missing velocity.
