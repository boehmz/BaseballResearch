                <tr id="so9" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>The eighth overall selection in the 2015 draft after a strong college career at Vanderbilt, Fulmer rose quickly through the minors. The right-handed starter threw just 22 innings in A-ball before opening ’16 in Double-A. He then earned an eight-game promotion to the majors — as a reliever — before heading to Triple-A to finish off the year back in the starting rotation. Despite a modest build, Fulmer has all the makings of a number two or three starter if he can polish his command and control. He walked 63 batters in 114.2 minor league innings in 2016 and added another seven free passes in 11.2 big league innings. His lack of command was also evident in his debut as he went from allowing just eight homers in the minors to two in a very small sample underscoring the fact that velocity isn’t everything. As the off-season showed us, the Sox are in full rebuilding mode. Fulmer, 23, isn’t currently earmarked for a starting gig but a trade of Jose Quintana could change that - or a strong spring with improved command/control. The most likely scenario, though, has the young hurler opening 2017 in Triple-A and moving back up to the majors around mid-season. There is a small chance that he could continue to gain experience in the majors as a reliever — given the lack of quality depth in the bullpen — but that would probably hurt Fulmer’s development more than it would help since he needs innings to iron out his issues. (Marc Hulet)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Fulmer ascended quickly through the minors as a 2015 first-round pick. He’ll likely need more experience in the minors before being called upon to start for the rebuilding White Sox - although the club promoted Carlos Rodon before his command/control was big-league ready and allowed him to iron things out in the majors. Still, don’t expect Fulmer to be ready to contribute to your squad until mid-season.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
