He was third in the majors and first in the American League in strikeouts, while adding only 1.8 walks per nine to go with the strikeouts. The lack of defense helped produce a .323 batting average on balls in play (career high) and pump Sale's ERA (3.41) over his ERA estimators, which were around 2.50. If the White Sox make any kind of improvement on offense and defense -- and it looks like they have -- his overall results will only get better. After not allowing a homer to lefties for three straight years and just three in his first 809 PA against them (0.4%), they clubbed five in 135 PA last year (4%). Fenway Park is one of the stingiest in the league for lefty homers and should allowed Sale to get back to his lefty-destroying ways.
