                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>"Folty" has a tantalizing mix of pitches, throwing a curve at 75 mph, a slider at 85 mph and a four-seamer and sinker that come in around 95 mph. He gets whiffs on the slider and curve at roughly the same rate, right around 14%. It’s a great starting point for a pitcher, but he needs to figure out a way to consistently get lefties out. They touched him for a .401 weighted on base average last year, and the only pitch that he didn’t have a .360 or higher batting average against was his four-seamer. Lefties smashed him all over the yard. They slugged .680 or better on every pitch outside of the four-seamer. A move to the pen may be in the cards for Folty. He throws hard, and has a big velo gap between pitches. He can pick either the slider or curve -- I’d lean to the curve -- and pair that with a fastball that will tick up and sniff 100 on the reg. He could be a monster reliever and he only has the soon-to-be-traded Jason Grilli and Arodys Vizcaino to contend with. He should be starting the year in Gwinnett, and if that’s the case, pay attention to him. If he’s starting and figures out lefties, he’d be a decent pickup. (Darren Schienbein)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Foltynewicz may be on the outside looking in when it comes to the Atlanta rotation to start 2016.
