He can dial his heater up into the upper-90s and, unlike a lot of hard-throwers, he actually has pretty decent control (just 35 walks in 109.1 Triple-A innings). He struggled with consistency in his 11 big league appearances due to a lack of fastball command and too many walks (22 in 44 innings). An improved changeup could also help him against left-handed hitters. He allowed just four home runs in his debut but all of them came against the opposite-side hitters (in just 25.1 combined innings). Because of mechanical issues, there has been some talk of moving Lopez to the bullpen permanently - where his fastball could break 100 mph more consistently. There might be something to the idea; in his MLB debut he held hitters to a .170 batting average with men on base so the added pressure may help him focus better. With his new organization, though, he'll no doubt be given every opportunity to try and develop into a number two or three starter - especially if Jose Quintana is headed out of town. If the veteran hurler stays, it increases the likelihood that Lopez will open the year in Triple-A to work on further polishing his game.
