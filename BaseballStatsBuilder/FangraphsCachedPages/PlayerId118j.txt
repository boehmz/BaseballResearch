Albers spent most of the season in the minors, and was unimpressive. He did make it to the big leagues and made one appearance for the Blue Jays, a two-and-two-thirds inning showing in which he faced 11 batters, struck out one, walked two, and gave up a bomb. That was it for Albers in the majors in 2015. Albers had been in the Twins' system for years and actually started 10 games in the majors in 2013. On the surface, there were good elements: he did not walk many batters and kept his ERA around four. But a 10% strikeout rate will not cut it in the big leagues, and his ground ball rate was not good enough to justify keeping him around. Albers spent 2014 playing in Korea, and obviously some teams are interested in checking out soft-tossing lefties for some sort of use. Albers is a free agent as of this writing, but even if he gets picked up, it is difficult to imagine him having any value on draft day.
