                <tr id="so7" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>So… 2014 didn’t quite go as planned for Mr. Heaney. After slicing his way through the minors, missing a ton of bats in the process, Heaney’s first go around at the major league level had its fair share of speed bumps. Heaney’s struggles came down to two things: allowing too much contact and home runs. Scouts have long predicted that Heaney’s stuff, while good, isn’t of the swing-and-miss type. And when that’s the case, missing spots can become deadly. He missed a ton of spots. We’re dealing with a small sample size, but there are still some good signs to go along with the bad ones: Heaney’s slider produced a 27% swinging strike rate; His change-up generated a 73% ground-ball rate; His fastball, though, registered a whopping 30% home run to fly ball ratio; that should come down. As with any young pitcher, Heaney is going to have growing pains. His changeup is well thought of in scouting circles, so hopefully it allows him to keep right-handed batters in check going forward, allowing him to miss a few more bats in the process. His recent move to the American League dampers things a bit, but his new home park is still rather friendly towards pitchers. Heaney should grow in 2014, but he must limit hard contact a better to become a useful option for fantasy owners. (Landon Jones)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Heaney’s learning on the fly, and his minor league track record suggests he should be at least serviceable in short time. But for 2015, it feels best to take a wait-and-see approach in most leagues, rather than investing too heavily on draft day.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>The ninth overall pick in the 2012 draft, Heaney has been on basically every top 100 prospect list since he was originally chosen by the Marlins. Three years and two trades later, we've finally gotten a chance to see him in significant action during 2015, and the results -- as we might expect out of any hyped prospect -- had their highs and lows. Overall, his minor league peripherals translated well to the increased rigors of the majors, as he maintained his excellent walk rates and posted respectable strikeout numbers. Closer inspection does reveal some significant second half struggles, as his strikeout minus walk rate was cut in half when compared to his shortened first half by an erosion in both categories. Most 24-year-old pitchers are going to have their struggles, though, so we should reasonably expect some duds in the game log while he continues to adjust to the league. Still, the talent and stuff are there to succeed as a mid-rotation starter, and much of realizing that potential will hinge on developing his changeup to deal with his platoon split, which was significant (.250 weighted on base average vs. lefties as opposed to a .315 wOBA vs. righties). The floor is high enough for Heaney to remain as an above average starter even if his mostly minor 2015 growing pains continue -- the main question in 2016 is how quickly he ascends toward his ceiling. (Owen Watson)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>The hype train is rolling after Heaney's 2015, which he started out by posting a sub-two ERA in his first meaningful 40 innings as a member of the Angels' rotation. Things weren't exactly smooth sailing after that, however, as he struggled in the second half. Hopefully that might depress his value a bit, as there is number three starter upside here -- especially in keeper and dynasty formats.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
