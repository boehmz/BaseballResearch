Carlos Martinez doesn't appear to be a candidate to find his way to failure, however. After having made all but one of his 68 career minor-league appearances as a starter, Martinez made all but one of his 33 appearances with the Cardinals in 2013 (including the postseason) as a reliever. The results were encouraging, as Martinez recorded better-than-average strikeout, walk, and ground-ball rates -- and posted better figures by all three measurements in the playoffs than regular season. Promising, that, all of it. Of some interest with regard to Martinez is the low-ish strikeout rate -- not relative to the league, of course, but to the quality of his repertoire, which he appears to use more to the end of inducing weak contact than swings and misses. Also of some interest is his future role. Probably a reliever in 2014 owing to the quality of the Cardinals' rotation, and perhaps the less-than-stellar quality of Martinez' change-up, the pitcher is talented and young enough to imagine the club entertaining the possibility of him starting in the future. (Carson Cistulli)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>A starter for the entirety of his minor-league career, Martinez pitched well in set-up for Trevor Rosenthal last year -- a role he's likely to reprise in 2014.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
        <tr id="so7" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Fantasy owners will want to keep a close eye on Martinez. Per research conducted by Eno Sarris and Daniel Schwartz, Martinez may possess one of the top overall pitch arsenals in the league. It's a four pitch mix with a 97 mph heater, sinker, slider, and changeup. While his 4.03 ERA (.333 batting average on balls in play) isn't awe inspiring, he maintained a solid strikeout rate (8.46 per nine) and devastating swinging strike rate (12.9%) over 89.1 innings. The sole blemish among his peripherals is a 3.63 walks per nine rate. A bigger cause for concern are his platoon splits. Against Martinez, lefties have a career .355 weighted on base average. Watch his use of the changeup in the spring if you're nervous about his splits. His role with the Cardinals is uncertain, but he's more likely to start than relieve. The indicators for a true breakout season are present. (Brad Johnson)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Martinez features, gas, a mostly full repertoire, and all the components of a standout pitcher. All that's missing are positive results. Martinez was a legitimate ace, but then, after a stressful start against the Cubs on that saw Martinez pitch 6.2 innings while striking out six, walking three, and giving up two runs, something was clearly wrong with Martinez at the beginning of his next start against the Brewers. The Cardinals shut Martinez down with solid totals for the season: 179.2 innings, 184 K, 63 BB, 3.01 ERA, and 3.21 FIP. If the injury had occurred in June, Martinez would have been back with the team a month later, but because it occurred at the end of the season, Martinez did not have the chance to prove his health. He has maintained an intense offseason regimen to strengthen his shoulder, and should do very well to prove to prove any doubters wrong. He gets there with the second-best velocity among qualified starters, plus an awesome sweeping, high-velocity breaking ball. He's slowly trusting his changeup -- with good movement and decent velocity gap despite averaging nearly 90 -- and that trust could help smooth out his splits and take away any remaining doubt that he's an ace. Everything looks great... except for a tendency to fly open and put stress on his arm, except for great velocity which puts more stress on his arm, and except for intermittent bouts with injury issues. Eh, what are you going to do. (Eno Sarris) <br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>The little we know about injury risk suggests that Carlos Martinez represents a high risk case.
