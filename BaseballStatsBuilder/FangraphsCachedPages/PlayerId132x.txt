He threw a career high 158 innings and in his first (and only) taste of Double-A, showed an ability to get strikeouts and limit home runs, without too many walks. In the early part of 2016 in Triple-A, he did the same - the walks went up a bit, but so did the strikeouts. The HR stayed low and he pitched his way into the MLB rotation. And the big leagues were not kind to Mr. Clevinger. He walked too many, gave up too many long balls, and basically just wasn't that good - until he moved to the pen. Now, Clevinger is not destined to be a relief arm, at least not yet, but in 12 innings as a reliever last year, he gave up not one HR, brought his walk rate down to 10.2% (vs. 13.0% as a starter), and generally demonstrated that he is quite capable of getting out MLB hitters. Working out as a starter as he preps for 2017, Clevinger now has to show that he can harness his bullpen effectiveness starting in the first inning. If he can, he could prove to be a solid mid-rotation SP.(<a href="https://twitter.com/chadyoung" style="color: #408725;">Chad Young</a>)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Clevinger has the tools to succeed against MLB hitting but has not yet put it all together as a SP.
