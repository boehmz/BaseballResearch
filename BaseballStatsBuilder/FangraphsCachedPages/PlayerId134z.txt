Perdomo had a four-seam and a two-seam at some point in 2015, but then someone must have noticed that the sinker was superior in every way -- movement, relative velocity, whiffs, grounders, line drives, and home runs off the pitch were all equal to or better than the four-seamer's efforts -- and suddenly the four-seam was almost gone and Perdomo was pounding two-seamers with regularity. We don't always know that a good pitch thrown more often becomes a great pitch, but in the case of Perdomo, he retained all his excellent peripherals on the pitch as he threw it more. In August, he started throwing the splitter more to complement the curve. That gave him two months with a 4.20 ERA and a 61% ground ball rate. He still only had 5.6 strikeouts per nine in that stretch -- neither the curve nor the splitter is a plus pitch -- but even those mediocre results are useful in today's game. Buy Perdomo wherever real-life usefulness on dollar-bin prices is interesting. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Perdomo has one great pitch -- a sinker.
