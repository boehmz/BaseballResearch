You'll probably hear a lot of different opinions on him. Some will focus on the velocity -- if the Jacob de Grom hair clone had thrown 100 more innings, he would have had top-20 velocity among 94 starters above that threshold. But in three of his past six starts, he barely cracked 93 mph as an average, and he may not really be a plus-velocity guy. Some may want to focus on his arsenal -- and it's true that his sinker and cutter were well above average by whiffs and grounders. But there's no third average or above-average pitch, as his curve and change are uninspiring. Some will point to pedigree -- he didn't have much of it, and he just put up the best strikeout rate of his career in the big leagues. There are a ton of plusses and minuses here, so it's hard to pick one reason he'll be bad or good. I resort to some intuition here. I get a bit of a Tanner Roark vibe -- decent pitches, no real standout out pitch, and good sinker. Take a look at Roark's career and you'll see that this *still* doesn't say that next year's Gsellman is a lock to be any better than the pedestrian projections. It just means that Gsellman will have other good years in his future. He's probably a better dynasty asset than 2017 redraft pickup, given owners will be paying for last year's version. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>If Gsellman can take a step forward with the curve and/or changeup next year, he'll move from being more of a kitchen sink guy to having more promise.
