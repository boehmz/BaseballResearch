He pitched okay, and contributed 100 innings, but he didn’t produce big numbers with a strikeout rate of just 6.66. His success comes not from overpowering hitters but by throwing a ton of strikes, commanding four pitches and inducing a lot of ground balls. He currently sits somewhere between seventh and ninth on the club’s starting depth chart so his best hope for breaking camp with the club may come via the bullpen as the long man. There’s probably not much to see here from the fantasy perspective unless Stripling finds his way to a new organization. And even then, he’s not the type of hurler that’s going to produce big fantasy numbers.
