Since then, Nolasco has been better than his ERA has suggested, and the Twins bet on him... and have lost. While Nolasco still puts up solid WAR numbers compared to his ERA, it comes thanks to what we would normally call “bad luck” on balls put into play. The Twins will try to sell someone on taking on Nolasco -- or at least part of his contract -- but will inevitably be unable to, and thus will be forced to try to showcase him early in the year.
