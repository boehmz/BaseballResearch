After that season, his home run totals have been 9, 11, and 11 in order. The problem with the power hasn't been his home run per fly ball rate, which has always hovered around 14%-15%. The problem has been his inability to make contact (33% strikeout rate) and put the ball in the air (1.5 ground balls per fly ball). Ten home runs is probably about right for a 2015 projection.  Additionally, his average is on a four-year decline (.295 to .243 to .227 to .218) which corresponds to a four-year rise in his strikeout rate (24% to 24% to 30% to 33%).  With some regression, a .230 average with a 30% K% should be the projection here. He hasn't stolen a base in two seasons, so expect none in 2015. The biggest issue with Avila will be his health after missing days to concussions the last two seasons. Even healthy, though, a .230/10/0 catcher is not that valuable in most leagues.
