                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>There are few starting pitchers whose health is more in question heading into the 2016 season than Jesse Hahn. After missing half a season at the end of 2015 with vague arm problems -- elbow inflammation and forearm tightness among them -- it's anyone's guess how many innings he'll be good for in Oakland's starting rotation this season. While ligament damage has been ruled out, Hahn already has had one Tommy John surgery (2010), and the A's have been very active in signing depth into their rotation during the 2015 offseason in case he is in fact injured. However, Hahn is also an intriguing risk / reward option, given his great ground ball ability and solid strikeout / walk rates. He has also shown an intriguing propensity (albeit in a small sample) to limit hard contact, as his 23.3% hard-hit rate against was fifth-lowest among starters in 2015 (minimum 90 innings pitched). He could easily thrive with that sort of makeup in the spacious Oakland Coliseum, and everything depends on whether the rest he's had since his injuries in July of 2015 have fully gotten him over his arm trouble. Let's hope so: there's fantastic upside if he can stay healthy. (Owen Watson)  <br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>A promising arm, Hahn pairs elite ground ball rates with strikeout upside when he's at his best. Unfortunately, he missed the entire second half of the season in 2015, and the size of his workload in 2016 is anything but clear. It might be worth taking a gamble on Hahn's upside, but be prepared for the possibility that he misses significant time if his arm troubles continue.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
                <tr id="so9" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Jesse Hahn had a weird 2016. After two seasons where he combined serious fantasy upside with frustrating injuries, Hahn came into 2016 perfectly healthy, by all accounts, and even with a newly sped-up fastball (93.9 MPH in 2016 vs. 92 MPH the year before). Unfortunately, pretty much everything else went wrong. Hahn only pitched nine games in the majors, during which he couldn't get bats to miss (6.0% swinging strike rate); outside of the zone, in particular, his swing rate dropped to 26.1% and his contact rate rose to 80.8%. Relatedly, his K and BB rates both ended up around 10%, contributing to a 5.63 FIP and a 5.37 SIERA. Hahn bounced between the majors and AAA for most of the season, including a DL stint that kept him out for most of August. At his best, Hahn combines strong groundball tendencies with a decent number of strikeouts, but it remains to be seen if he can regain his previous form -- and stay healthy -- in 2017. Keep an eye on him but don't add him to your roster with any expectations. (Brice Russ)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>After two promising, but injury-riddled seasons, Hahn stayed active for most of 2016 but saw a serious drop in performance.
