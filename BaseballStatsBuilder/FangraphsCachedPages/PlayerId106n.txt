                <tr id="so6" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Danny Duffy has quite a bit of potential, but his lack of control may derail his fantasy value. First, this will be his first complete season after having Tommy John surgery in 2012. He was able to throw a decent number of strikeouts (8.1 strikeouts per nine in the majors). He has two inter-connected issues limiting his potential. First, he walks too many batters (4.75 walks per nine in the majors). A pitcher is rarely going to make it in the big leagues when they put an extra runner on base every other inning. The walks lead to his second limitation, pitch inefficiency. In five 2013 starts, he averaged 4.9 innings. It was not like he was on a super-short pitch count either -- he averaged 94 pitches per start. He did improve a bit from 2012 when he was at 4.5 innings per start. If he can't even pitch five innings, he can't be awarded a win. Going into 2014, I would be look to pick Duffy up on the cheap. Follow him early to see if he starts throwing more strikes and enjoy the breakout. If he doesn't, dump him and move on. Keeping him will not be worth the roller coaster ride. (Jeff Zimmerman)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Danny Duffy has the potential to be a number two starter if he can just throw more strikes. Until then, he will just be another high-strikeout, high-walk pitcher.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
                <tr id="so7" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Here's the weird thing about Danny Duffy's 2014 season: despite putting up a 2.53 ERA in nearly 150 innings, he didn't have a single plus pitch by pitch-type peripherals. Well, maybe you can give his fastball (8% whiffs, 6.4% is average) that moniker, it does hum along at 94 mph, so that's not surprising. But his change, slider, and big ole curve -- none of those pitches rated as above average by whiffs or grounders. That's how you get a below-average strikeout rate out of what looks like a plus package on the mound. This year, he finally put up a walk that was better than average, but the difference between his strikeout and walk rates was below average, and his ground-ball rate was terrible. If he regresses with the command -- his career major- and minor-league walk rates suggest he will -- then it will be hard to love him in 2015. The pop-ups are nice (6% career, average is 3.7%) but without plus command will he be able to continue to hit those spots? And even if he does get another 20+ popups next year, will it be enough to over come his bad strikeout and walk rates? Duffy is risky. His upside is mixed-league worthy, but don't overspend. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>A pop-up inducing rising 94 mph fastball is a thing to build a career upon. The fact that Duffy's command is unstable and his other pitches don't get whiffs or grounders eats away at that foundation, though. Be careful with this 25-year-old.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Danny Duffy’s 2016 value is a hard to pin down at this point. As a starter he has been maybe an okay fourth or fifth starter in the real life game. His 8.1% strikeout minus walk rate in 2014 and 2015 as a starter ranks 93rd of 100 among starters with a minimum of 250 innings pitched. His one saving grace has been his fly ball nature (only 39% grounders) and the Royals elite outfield defense behind him. Since 2012, his ERA has been significantly lower than his other ERA estimators each season. His career ERA is 3.80 with compared to a 4.22 FIP, 4.55 xFIP and 4.48 SIERA. I don’t see anything is his profile that points to a major change in 2016. As a starter, I see his Steamer projection being dead on with a ~4.00 ERA, 1.40 WHIP and 6.9 strikeouts per nine. If this level in talent is useful in your league, feel free to play him. One interesting note on Duffy was his late season move to the bullpen. In only 16 innings of work he posted a 12.4 K/9 with a fastball averaging 96 mph (compared to his 94 mph season average). It will be interest to see if he becomes the Royals' next bad starter turned great reliever. The problem is that, with Wade Davis as the closer and several over good relievers on the depth chart (Kelvin Herrera and Luke Hochevar), he will be blocked from save opportunities even if he does end up in the bullpen. (Jeff Zimmerman)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Danny Duffy is a rosterable starter for AL-only and 20-team or deeper leagues. He has the potential to be a nice closer, but he would need to contend with the rest of the Royals' elite bullpen.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>

He found the confidence to throw the ball in the zone with his nastier stuff as well, resulting in a career-high zone percentage. Duffy doesn't have better command now, but throwing a sinker more often allowed him to throw to the middle of the zone, particularly for a first-pitch strike. Finally, throwing the changeup more often has helped improve its drop and make it into a legitimate third weapon. It's quite the turnaround, so everyone will be nervous. But it's important to remember that he's always had this stuff, and that we always wondered why he didn't get better results. This version probably makes more sense if you think about a lefty with a 95 mph fastball, a nasty slurve, and now a strong changeup. Go get the bear suit, Danny.
