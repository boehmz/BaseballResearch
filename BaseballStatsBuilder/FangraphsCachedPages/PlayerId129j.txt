Conley made 11 starts with four relief appearances and posted a decent strikeout rate and adequate walk rate in 67 innings. While in a small sample, he performed better as a starter than as a reliever, with 55 strikeouts in 58 innings. He has decent low-90's velocity with two fastballs and offers a slider and changeup from the left side. He performed better against righties than lefties, which combined with his better performance as a starter leads one to believe the back end of the rotation may make more sense for him than a bullpen role. Still, nothing is set in stone as to his role next year, but it is more likely than not that Conley will see at least some, and as many as a full season's worth, of starts next year. If he is able to emulate his success as a rookie, he could actually be useful in standard formats as a back end rotation option. He lacks significant upside, but he is someone to monitor throughout spring training and the early parts of the season. In his followup season last year, he improved his changeup in depth and velocity gap but also lost some fastball velocity and suffered with bouts of wildness. Now he looks like a pitcher with three good pitches and a nice home park, yes, but he also has command concerns that make him about league average when seen in sum.
