He was third in the majors and first in the American League in strikeouts, while adding only 1.8 walks per nine to go with the strikeouts. The lack of defense helped produce a .323 batting average on balls in play (career high) and pump Sale's ERA (3.41) over his ERA estimators, which were around 2.50. If the White Sox make any kind of improvement on offense and defense -- and it looks like they have -- his overall results will only get better.
