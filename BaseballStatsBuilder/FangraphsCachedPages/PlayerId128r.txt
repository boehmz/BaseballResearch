                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>A college arm out of UVA, Tyler Wilson put up solid, if unspectacular, numbers as he climbed the rungs through the Baltimore farm system. The right-hander finally got the call to make his big league debut in 2015 and he ended up posting a 3.50 ERA over 36 innings (nine appearances, five starts). His FIP was a tidy 3.77, but xFIP and SIERA don't buy the tiny home run rate, pushing marks near 5.00. A large part of Wilson's minor league success (sub-4.00 FIPs at every step except for his first tastes in A and Double-A ball) was being able to put up strikeout per walk marks north of 3.0, but his small sample major league mark last year was only 1.2. With a fastball that barely cracks 90 mph and average (at best) offspeed offerings, it is no surprise that his swinging strike rate was only 5%. He's likely destined for a long relief/spot start role if he opens 2016 with the big club. But, given the fact that he has options, he seems more likely to be ticketed to Triple A as pitching depth. While his decent minor league rates (and the fact that I'm a sucker for unheralded guys who turn out to be useful pitchers in their late 20s) make him a mildly interesting depth stash in deeper dynasty leagues, he is likely to be (at best) a mid-season streamer if he makes a couple starts for the O's.
