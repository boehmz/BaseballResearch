The 91-loss Marlins can probably afford to have a young asset like Urena find his way in the majors, but even Urena’s minor league track record suggests there is little upside to him in fantasy. His 57% first pitch strike rate is 14th-worst among 200 pitchers with at least 140 innings the last two seasons and the consistent traffic on the bases (1.50 WHIP) has led to a 64% left-on-base rate, well below the 73% league average. Perhaps relief is the answer, but it's worth noting that the first inning is his worst: .884 OPS and 20 runs in 96 PA, though that doesn't automatically mean he'd struggle in a one-inning relief stint. On the plus side, Urena is just 25 years old so there's definitely time for him to parlay his raw stuff into some success, regardless of role. His only fantasy viability is for you diehards playing in deep NL-only dynasties where anyone with a pulse is worthwhile.
