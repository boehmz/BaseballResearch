                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>When you think of players who have burst onto the big league stage and made an impact for their club, you should think of Lance McCullers. With just four starts above High-A under his belt, McCullers was called upon to fill a spot in the Astros rotation, and in his fourth start as a major leaguer, the right-hander threw a complete game, striking out 11. On the year, McCullers managed to strike out three times as many batters as he walked, something he struggled to do in the minors despite big strikeout rates. McCullers works with fastball that sits in the mid 90s, a hard curveball that ranks among the best in baseball, and a changeup that works in the high-80s to low-90s. In 22 big league starts last year, McCullers pitched like a frontline starter, and he has the stuff to back it up. He may take some lumps after teams have had an offseason to prepare for him, and he’ll be on an innings limit in 2016, forcing fantasy owners to take pause if they’re playing in head-to-head formats. Even if he only goes 170 innings, McCullers has the potential to be a top 30 fantasy starter, and taking him as your number three is not a bad bet. (Zach Sanders)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Lance McCullers has a big fastball and one of baseball’s better breaking balls. Only a devastating 84 mph curve that looks like the one Boston closer Craig Kimbrel throws, except the Astro throws it 40 times a game and it still gets whiffs one out of every five times. Third pitch? Only a devastating split finger that doesn't have good velocity gap, but fades and drops more than four inches more than his four-seamer. Command? Late in his work last year, McCullers started throwing two-seamers on the first pitch in order to have the confidence to throw it to the middle of the zone and let the movement take it to the corners. That last answer may not be as complete as the others, and there's still one question that cannot be answered -- will he be healthy this year? If he answers those last two questions well, though, McCullers will be an ace's ace. If he only answers one, he'll either be a hurt ace or a great source of strikeouts. Seems like a decent bet for your second or third starter. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Start with blazing stuff -- velocity, movement, separation in spades -- and hope McCullers answers the health and command questions this season.
