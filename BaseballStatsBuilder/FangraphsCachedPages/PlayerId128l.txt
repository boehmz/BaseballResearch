He has a strong high-80s changeup that kept lefties at bay and stifled righties even more (albeit at a much lower usage rate) and even added a hard slider by the end of the year. Syndergaard was seen as a potential frontline starter coming up in the minors and his 2015 debut only reaffirmed that notion. His walk rate was actually better than advertised at 5%, down from 7% in 456 minor league innings. Homers were a bit of an issue, but didn't kill him because he allowed so few base runners. He allowed at least one homer in each of his six August starts and only had one homer-free outing in his last 10 starts. He throws a ton of strikes with the fastball because it routinely draws whiffs, but he needs to command it more. It surrendered 13 of his 19 homers, usually flattening out a bit and getting stuck in that happy zone in the middle of the plate. It's a problem not uncommon for young power arms (see also: Salazar, Danny), but it's definitely fixable as he starts refining his command. If you're unwilling to pay a top-60 or so pick for Thor, you're going to look silly carrying that hammer around in the draft when he winds up on someone else's team. (<a href="http://thespguide.com/">Paul Sporer</a>)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Syndergaard delivered on huge prospect hype and already finds himself firmly in the top-20 starting pitchers heading into 2016.
