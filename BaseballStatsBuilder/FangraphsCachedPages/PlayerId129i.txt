                <tr id="so8" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Vincent Velasquez has great stuff. A 94 mph rising fastball. A sizzling, 82 mph power curve. An 85 mph slider that he doesn't use a ton but got whiffs nearly a third of the time last year. And then there's that change that he doesn't yet trust fully but has six inches of drop beyond his four-seam, and almost four inches more fade, while being eight mph slower. All of those things are good. The change, in a small sample, was more good than above average, and the curve didn't wow by whiffs. He also has iffy command, and some think he's headed for the bullpen. But now that he's in Philadelphia, that team is going to give him every chance to start. Sure, he might start in the minors at first, but there's nobody holding him back. Velasquez is a great draft-day pickup in any league that will allow you to hold on to him for a month or two until he's starting in the bigs. If your league is too small for that, just file him away for a July pickup. He's very exciting. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>If only his projections were worse, there might not be a better sleeper in baseball this year. As is, it looks like everyone will be in on Velasquez. Just remember he may not break camp with the team, and you may have to wait a month or two to see that sizzling stuff in Philadelphia.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
                <tr id="so9" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Velasquez is not a standout pitcher by spin rates -- his curve and fastball are just a tick above average in that regard -- but otherwise there's a lot to love about his stuff. He's got above-average rise on a fastball that's a mile per hour above average for a righty, great movement on a hard change, a slider with average-ish results, and a curve with a lot of drop for averaging 79 mph. That means double-digit whiffs on every pitch he throws, and a whiff rate in the top 30 over the last two years (minimum 180 innings thrown). The arsenal absolutely has what it takes to produce a starting pitcher, even if his secondary stuff is a little homer-prone -- that's not in question here. The question is if he can stay healthy as a starter. His high-effort, high-velocity ways produced a biceps strain last year that cost him nearly a month, and there have always been whispers about the future of his ligaments. In a one-year situation, the upside of all those strikeouts paired with any improvement in home run rate is worth a mid-rotation chunk of change. After all, he was top-10 in strikeout percentage last year. In dynasty leagues, buying low is an okay idea, but that sort of thing is near impossible in those kinds of leagues, and maybe the health concern means you should just wish him and his owner good luck. (Eno Sarris)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Velasquez will provide you gas and whiffs (and also health risk) in equal, devastating amounts. The former means we'll all step up to the trough to some extent, but the latter means he won't cost as much as other top pitching sleepers. For good reason.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
