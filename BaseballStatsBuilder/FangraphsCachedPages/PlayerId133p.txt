Of particular note is one number that he cites: 78. That's the top velocity, in miles per hour, at which Strahm threw as a high-school pitcher. He was a soft-tossing, small (6-foot, 150-pound), cold-weather (North Dakota) player. It's not surprising, in other words, that he wasn't particularly coveted as a prep prospect. A combination of better conditioning and cleaner mechanics, however, has transformed the junior-college product and former 21st-round pick into a legitimate major leaguer. In a 22-inning debut for the Royals last season, Strahm struck out over a third of the batters he faced. That success, and the minor league success that preceded it, has earned Strahm a place in the Royals' Opening Day bullpen at the very least. There's no reason to think he wouldn't manage fine in the club's rotation, as well. For the moment, however, Kansas City seems committed to a different path.
