                <tr id="so9" style="background-color: #FFF; display:none;"><td colspan="5" padding:3px; text-align:justify;"><div class="m_stories" style="font-size:10pt"><center><table><tr><td style="width:615px; padding-right:7px; text-align:justify;" valign="top"><b>Profile: </b>Cuban native Miranda made his big league debut in 2016 with the Orioles but appeared in just one game before making his way to Seattle. He appeared in another 11 games (10 starts) and pitched relatively well although he was hurt by the home run (12 in just 56 innings). Home runs may always been an issue for the lefty unless he makes some adjustments to his approach; he's an extreme fly-ball pitcher. He has slightly-above-average stuff with a heater that averaged just shy of 93 mph in '16. His lack of a reliable and true breaking ball might be an issue as he relies on both a changeup and a splitter for his secondary offerings. Truth be told, he might be better off as a high-leverage reliever. He's no LOOGY, though, as he's had his struggles against southpaws. Miranda is still a bit of an enigma, having just signed his first pro contract in North America in 2015. Signs point to him being a decent back end starter, but he just might blossom as a reliever. Either way, he's probably not someone you want to consider for your fantasy team in April but things could change by mid-year depending on his usage. (Marc Hulet)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>A starter since coming to North America from Cuba two years ago, Miranda has good stuff for a lefty but his repertoire and results suggest a shift to the bullpen might really benefit him. For now, though, he appears to have a line on the fifth starter's job in Seattle - even with the addition of Yovani Gallardo.</td></tr></table></center></div></td></tr>
