With the acquisitions of Wade Miley and Nate Karns, Montgomery isn’t even in the running for a starting spot, barring a minor miracle; out of options, the lefty will either will be a reliever or subject to waivers, and his mediocre arsenal would not necessarily play up well in the pen. Now, he will enter the year as a candidate for the fifth starter role in Chicago, which could be a position filled with immense fantasy upside (see one Kyle Hendricks, 2016). Montgomery made five starts down the stretch for the Cubs as they wanted to rest some of their starting pitchers, which also gave him an opportunity to semi-audition for a starting role. His 2016 saw his strikeouts rise (along with this walks and home run rate), but Montgomery could be a very interesting late round addition in standard formats given his ballclubs propensity to win games and his strikeout potential. Being that he will likely not cost acquiring fantasy owners much on draft day, he is a good gamble to make if you are the type to wait on starting pitching. (Ben Duronio)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>The former sandwich round pick has the pedigree, skillset, and role to be a quality back end starter for the best team in baseball.
