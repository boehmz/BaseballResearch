Not only was he utilized exclusively in a relief capacity during his year-plus in the Cubs system, but when Kiley McDaniel spoke with team officials ahead of composing his Cubs list during the 2014-15 offseason, Godley received so little attention that McDaniel was compelled to exclude him from the list entirely. After moving to the Diamondbacks in the trade that sent Miguel Montero to Chicago, however, Godley immediately entered the rotation at High-A Visalia and produced strong strikeout and walk rates to complement his above average ground ball numbers. After a three start interval at Double-A Mobile, Godley made his major league debut in July, recording a 7:0 strikeout-to-walk ratio and conceding zero runs. He never matched that debut performance in any of his subsequent eight appearances for the parent club, but ultimately posted reasonably encouraging numbers. Pitchers who spend the majority of their age-25 seasons in the California League tend not to experience much success in the majors. That said, Godley appears to possess the ability both to create swings and misses and also manage contact. He's likely to receive major-league innings once again in 2016. One thing he excelled at in 2016 was getting ground balls. His ground ball rate was 53.8 percent. But with a higher ground ball rate comes a higher BABIP, and Godley was bitten by that in '16. He missed a decent amount of bats with an 11.6 SwStr%, but he also allowed 13 home runs in almost 75 innings. Playing in Arizona, that isn't terribly surprising. His four-pitch mix is intriguing -- 4-seamer, cutter, curveball, changeup -- but none of them are overpowering or even above-average. The Diamondbacks have some depth at starting pitcher, so Godley might not get a ton of looks in the rotation unless there are a few injuries. But he's still on their 40-man roster, so expect him to log some innings in 2017. (Dustin Nosler)<br /><br /><b>The Quick Opinion: </b>Godley profiles as a back-end starting pitcher or a fungible swingman. He doesn't do any one thing well, but he does get ground balls.
